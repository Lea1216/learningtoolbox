Golden Rules

Here you will find some golden rules you should follow when starting to design an online course or enhance your current courses.
Be concise

Identify core content of the lecture and weed out superfluous information because students' natural attention span for online courses is about 5-6 minutes.

Organize content into modules

Group content and exercises into thematic modules to prevent an online course from being confusing.

Activate

Use activities such as quizzes or discussion and compensate for the weaker relationship between educator and student in an online lecture. Make sure that your students learn actively.

Provide a channel for social interaction

Establish a social interaction channel to motivate your students to attend the course for its entire duration.

Manage expectations

Pretests and motivation letters help students to orient themselves and prepare for the course.

Give feedback and assess continously

Formative and constructive feedback, such as peer review or iterative tasks, effectively supports students in the learning process.
